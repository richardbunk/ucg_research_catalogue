# FTP-001 — Framework Transliteration & Reconstruction Plan

**Unified Composition Guide (UCG)**

**Title:** Framework Transliteration & Reconstruction Plan  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Operational Planning Standard  
**Authority Level:** Planning Document

---

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the operational strategy for reconstructing the current Unified Composition Guide (UCG) into its next-generation canonical architecture.

Rather than migrating existing framework artifacts directly, this plan defines a progressive editorial transliteration process through which institutional knowledge is evaluated, refined, modularized, and republished under the governance of the Research Catalogue.

Its purpose is to ensure that framework evolution remains evidence-based, modular, and architecturally coherent.

---

## 2. Governing Principle

The current operational UCG is not the final framework.

It is the principal institutional source corpus from which the next-generation UCG will be reconstructed.

Framework evolution shall occur through editorial refinement rather than direct migration.

---

## 3. Reconstruction Philosophy

Framework reconstruction preserves understanding rather than implementation.

Existing framework components shall be treated as research sources.

Each component shall undergo:

- editorial review
- knowledge extraction
- architectural evaluation
- modular reconstruction
- canonical publication

The objective is to preserve institutional knowledge while improving clarity, consistency, modularity, and long-term maintainability.

---

## 4. Strategic Objectives

The reconstruction effort seeks to:

- preserve accumulated institutional knowledge
- eliminate duplication
- normalize terminology
- improve architectural consistency
- establish modular canonical assets
- support repository-based operational deployment
- prepare the foundation for future UCG evolution

---

## 6. Transliteration Units

The unit of reconstruction is the conceptual framework component rather than the workbook or spreadsheet.

Examples include:

- individual style rules
- author personas
- context definitions
- behavioral patterns
- thematic concepts
- lexical entries
- governance principles

Each transliteration unit shall become an independently governed canonical asset.

---

## 7. Layered Authority Architecture

The reconstructed UCG shall operate as a layered authority system.

Each layer derives authority from the layer above while providing increasing specialization.

The conceptual authority stack is defined as:

```text
Layer 0 — Constitutional Core
Layer 1 — Core Framework
Layer 2 — Operational Extensions
Layer 3 — Domain Frameworks
Layer 4 — Persistent Knowledge
Layer 5 — Project Frameworks
Layer 6 — Runtime Context
```

Framework reconstruction shall assign every canonical component to the appropriate authority layer.

No component shall exist outside the authority architecture.

---

## 8. Operational Representation

Workbook-based indices are retained as planning and design artifacts.

Operational authority shall progressively transition to modular assets.

Canonical deployment formats include:

- Markdown (`.md`) for human-readable doctrine
- YAML (`.yaml`) for structured metadata and configuration
- JSON (`.json`) for operational indices and machine-readable data

Workbook formats remain valuable for:

- planning
- editorial design
- bulk analysis
- historical comparison

They shall no longer constitute the primary operational framework.

---

## 9. Transliteration Workflow

Every framework component shall follow the same editorial pathway established for external research.

```text
Framework Component
        ↓
Research Session
        ↓
Extraction
        ↓
Knowledge Article
        ↓
Framework Candidate
        ↓
Editorial Review
        ↓
Canonical Module
```

The current framework therefore enters the same editorial ecosystem as all future research.

---

## 10. Transliteration Outcomes

Following editorial review, each transliteration unit may result in one or more outcomes.

Possible outcomes include:

- retained without change
- editorial revision
- merged with another component
- divided into multiple canonical modules
- promoted to governance
- reassigned to another authority layer
- deprecated
- archived

Framework reconstruction is therefore editorial rather than mechanical.

---

## 11. Transliteration Priorities

Framework reconstruction shall generally proceed according to architectural dependency rather than document chronology.

Recommended priority:

1. Constitutional governance
2. Core framework
3. Operational extensions
4. Domain frameworks
5. Persistent knowledge
6. Project frameworks

This sequence establishes stable architectural foundations before higher-level specialization.

---

## 12. Completion Criteria

Framework reconstruction shall be considered operationally complete when:

- every framework component has undergone transliteration
- canonical modules have been published
- workbook dependencies have been retired from operational deployment
- modular authority layers have been established
- repository architecture reflects the canonical framework

Completion represents the successful reconstruction of the operational UCG rather than the end of framework evolution.

---

## 13. Continuous Reconstruction

Framework reconstruction is intended to be continuous.

Future research, editorial refinement, and operational experience shall introduce new transliteration opportunities throughout the lifecycle of the Unified Composition Guide.

The framework therefore evolves through continual institutional learning rather than periodic wholesale redesign.

Its purpose is to preserve accumulated institutional understanding while expressing that understanding within a modular, governed, layered authority architecture capable of continual refinement, operational deployment, and future growth.
