# RIP-001 — Research Ingestion & Framework Transliteration Protocol

**Unified Composition Guide (UCG) Research Catalogue**

**Document ID:** RIP-001  
**Title:** Research Ingestion & Framework Transliteration Protocol  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Operational Standard  
**Authority Level:** Operational Protocol

---

## 1. Purpose

This protocol establishes the operational workflow by which knowledge enters the Unified Composition Guide (UCG) Research Catalogue.

Unlike the RCG governance series, which defines constitutional principles, this protocol defines the day-to-day editorial process used to transform research into institutional knowledge.

The protocol applies equally to:

- external research sources
- the current operational Unified Composition Guide
- future revisions to existing framework components

Its objective is to ensure that all knowledge entering the Research Catalogue follows a consistent editorial workflow regardless of origin.

---

## 2. Governing Principle

Every framework component begins life as research.

No distinction is made between knowledge originating from external publications and knowledge originating from existing UCG framework components.

Both are subjected to the same editorial process.

This ensures that all institutional knowledge is evaluated, refined, and incorporated according to a single operational standard.

---

## 3. Operational Objectives

The protocol exists to:

- establish consistent research ingestion
- preserve editorial traceability
- eliminate duplicate workflows
- support progressive framework refinement
- create reusable institutional knowledge
- provide a repeatable pathway from source material to canonical framework components

---

## 4. Source Classes

The Research Catalogue recognizes two operational source classes.

### Class A — External Research Sources

Examples include:

- books
- academic papers
- manuals
- standards
- historical documents
- interviews
- websites
- technical references
- project postmortems

These sources introduce new knowledge into the ecosystem.

### Class B — Framework Sources

Examples include:

- current UCG operational framework
- Master Indices
- Style Kit
- Author Personas
- Context Matrix
- supplements
- registries
- governance documents
- templates
- existing knowledge collections

These sources represent institutional knowledge requiring editorial transliteration into the next-generation framework.

---

## 5. Source Registration

Every source entering the Research Catalogue shall first be registered.

Minimum registration information should include:

- Source Identifier
- Title
- Source Class
- Author or Origin
- Publication Date, if applicable
- Acquisition Method
- Current Status
- Priority
- Intended Research Scope

Registration establishes editorial traceability before research begins.

---

## 6. Research Session

Each registered source initiates a Research Session.

The purpose of a Research Session is to understand the source before extracting reusable knowledge.

Research Sessions should:

- identify the source's purpose
- establish editorial context
- identify major themes
- record initial observations
- determine extraction strategy

Research Sessions are exploratory rather than editorial.

---

## 7. Knowledge Extraction

Following familiarization with the source, structured extraction begins.

Extraction follows the governance established by RCG-002.

Editors identify:

- facts
- concepts
- systems
- framework opportunities

Extraction records preserve reusable observations without prematurely determining framework impact.

---

## 8. Knowledge Article Development

Extraction records are synthesized into Knowledge Articles.

Knowledge Articles should:

- integrate related observations
- remove unnecessary source dependence
- generalize where appropriate
- preserve technical accuracy
- support future editorial reuse

Knowledge Articles represent the primary editorial output of the Research Catalogue.

---

## 9. Framework Candidate Assessment

Knowledge Articles are evaluated for possible incorporation into the Unified Composition Guide.

Editorial outcomes include:

- institutional knowledge only
- framework enhancement
- new framework component
- governance recommendation
- future research recommendation

Only approved candidates continue toward framework integration.

---

## 10. Editorial Review

Framework Candidates undergo editorial review.

Review verifies:

- accuracy
- evidence
- consistency
- editorial quality
- architectural compatibility
- governance compliance

Editorial review confirms readiness for canonical consideration.

---

## 11. Progressive Framework Transliteration

The current Unified Composition Guide shall not be migrated directly into future versions.

Instead, existing framework components shall be treated as research sources.

Each component progresses through the same editorial workflow applied to external research.

This process is known as **Progressive Framework Transliteration**.

Its objectives are to:

- re-evaluate existing assumptions
- normalize terminology
- remove duplication
- improve editorial consistency
- strengthen architectural integration
- preserve valuable institutional knowledge

The next-generation Unified Composition Guide shall therefore emerge through editorial refinement rather than direct migration.

---

## 12. Unified Editorial Pipeline

All knowledge entering the Research Catalogue follows the same operational pathway.

```text
Source
    ↓
Registration
    ↓
Research Session
    ↓
Extraction
    ↓
Knowledge Article
    ↓
Framework Candidate
    ↓
Editorial Review
    ↓
Canonical Framework Component
```

The source's origin does not alter the workflow.

Only its content determines its eventual contribution.

---

## 13. Operational Deliverables

Each completed research effort may produce one or more of the following:

- Source Record
- Research Session Record
- Extraction Records
- Knowledge Articles
- Framework Candidates
- Editorial Review Record
- Publication Recommendation

Not every source is expected to generate every deliverable.

---

## 14. Completion Criteria

A source has completed operational ingestion when:

- registration is complete
- research session is concluded
- extraction is complete
- Knowledge Articles have been authored where appropriate
- Framework Candidates have been identified
- editorial review requirements have been satisfied

Completion signifies that the source has been fully incorporated into the Research Catalogue.

---

## 15. Relationship to Governance

The operational workflow defined by this protocol is governed by the Research Catalogue governance series.

Specifically:

- RCG-001 establishes the purpose of the Research Catalogue.
- RCG-002 governs research extraction and framework codification.
- RCG-003 governs information architecture.
- RCG-004 governs editorial quality.

RIP-001 implements these governance principles in daily editorial practice.

---

## Operational Statement

> Every source deserves consistent editorial treatment. Whether knowledge originates from a newly acquired research publication or from the existing Unified Composition Guide itself, it enters a common editorial workflow designed to preserve understanding, improve quality, and progressively construct the next generation of the Unified Composition Guide through evidence-based refinement.
