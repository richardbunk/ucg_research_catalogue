# RCG-003 — UCG Research Catalogue v1.0 Operational Protocol

**Status:** Adopted  
**Applies To:** UCG Research Catalogue v1.0  
**Effective State:** Design Freeze

---

## Purpose

This protocol establishes the operational discipline governing the initial publication and population of the UCG Research Catalogue.

The objective of v1.0 is to establish a complete, stable, and usable research environment that supports reconstruction of the next generation of the Unified Composition Guide (UCG).

The repository itself is not the ultimate objective.

The repository exists to accelerate evidence-based framework reconstruction.

---

## Governing Principle

The Research Catalogue is a means to an end.

Its success is measured by how effectively it enables research, synthesis, and framework reconstruction, not by the sophistication of its own governance.

Accordingly, governance shall remain intentionally stable throughout the v1.0 publication cycle.

---

## Design Freeze

Upon adoption of this protocol, the architectural design of the repository is considered frozen for Version 1.0.

No new governance structures, document classes, workflows, metadata systems, or repository conventions shall be introduced unless they prevent completion of the current publication cycle.

Operational consistency takes precedence over architectural perfection.

---

## Deferred Improvements

Ideas for improvement are expected to emerge during practical use.

These observations shall be documented, preserved, and assigned to a future review log, but shall not modify the active v1.0 governance.

All proposed improvements become candidates for Version 1.1 review after the initial publication cycle has been completed.

---

## Operational Priorities

Until Version 1.0 is complete, project effort shall prioritize:

1. Publishing governance documents.
2. Completing repository infrastructure.
3. Publishing operational templates and registers.
4. Populating the repository with source materials.
5. Performing research extraction and assessment.
6. Building concept clusters.
7. Producing framework components.
8. Populating the Framework Manifest.
9. Supporting reconstruction of UCG vNext.

Repository refinement is not considered productive work unless it directly enables one of these objectives.

---

## Evidence Before Optimization

During Version 1.0, practical experience shall be considered more valuable than theoretical refinement.

Workflow changes should arise from demonstrated operational need rather than anticipated improvement.

The repository should first prove that it functions.

Only afterward should it be optimized.

---

## Versioning Policy

Version 1.0 establishes the constitutional and operational baseline.

Version 1.1 is reserved for evidence-supported refinement.

Version 2.0 is reserved for structural or architectural evolution that materially changes repository operation.

---

## Completion Criteria

Version 1.0 shall be considered complete when:

- Governance documents have been published.
- Repository infrastructure has been established.
- Operational standards have been published.
- Templates and registers are available.
- Source intake is functioning.
- Research, assessment, cataloguing, and synthesis workflows have been demonstrated.
- Framework Components are being produced.
- The Framework Manifest has begun to populate.
- The repository is actively supporting reconstruction of UCG vNext.

---

## Operational Doctrine

The repository shall remain focused on its intended mission.

Governance exists to enable research.

Research exists to produce framework components.

Framework components exist to reconstruct the Unified Composition Guide.

At no point should development of the repository itself become the primary project.

The completion of UCG vNext remains the ultimate measure of the repository's success.
