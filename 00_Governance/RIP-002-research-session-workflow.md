---

1. Purpose

This protocol defines the standard workflow for conducting a Research Session within the Unified Composition Guide (UCG) Research Catalogue.

A Research Session is the primary operational activity through which editors become familiar with a source before identifying reusable knowledge.

The objective of a Research Session is understanding.

Extraction follows understanding.

---

2. Governing Principle

Editors should seek to understand a source before attempting to extract reusable knowledge from it.

Research Sessions therefore prioritize comprehension, context, and editorial judgement over immediate codification.

---

3. Relationship to Governance

This protocol implements the governance established by:

- RCG-002 — Research Extraction & Framework Codification Protocol
- RCG-003 — Research Asset & Information Architecture Standard
- RCG-004 — Research Editorial Standards

Research Sessions represent the operational beginning of the editorial pipeline defined by RIP-001.

---

4. Session Inputs

Every Research Session begins with a registered source.

Typical inputs include:

- books
- academic papers
- manuals
- historical works
- interviews
- websites
- project documentation
- current UCG framework components

Each session should reference an existing Source Record.

---

5. Research Objectives

Before beginning a session, the editor should establish the intended scope of investigation.

Typical objectives include:

- general familiarization
- targeted research
- framework validation
- transliteration of an existing UCG component
- investigation of a specific concept
- editorial verification

Clearly defining the objective helps guide the depth and focus of the session.

---

6. Session Workflow

A Research Session generally progresses through the following stages.

Preparation
        ↓
Initial Reading
        ↓
Context Development
        ↓
Analytical Reading
        ↓
Observation Capture
        ↓
Session Summary
        ↓
Extraction Planning

The workflow emphasizes understanding before editorial synthesis.

---

7. Preparation

Prior to reading, the editor should:

- review the Source Record
- establish session objectives
- identify expected areas of interest
- determine the intended scope of review

Preparation provides editorial direction without prejudging conclusions.

---

8. Initial Reading

The first reading should emphasize broad understanding.

Editors should seek to understand:

- purpose
- audience
- structure
- terminology
- central arguments
- recurring themes

The objective is orientation rather than detailed analysis.

---

9. Context Development

Editors should identify the broader context surrounding the source.

Context may include:

- historical background
- technical discipline
- intended use
- relationship to existing UCG knowledge
- potential framework relevance

Context assists later editorial interpretation.

---

10. Analytical Reading

Subsequent review should identify areas of enduring value.

Editors should observe:

- recurring principles
- methodologies
- conceptual patterns
- systems
- terminology
- framework opportunities

Observations remain provisional until extraction.

---

11. Observation Capture

Observations should be recorded in a structured manner.

Typical observations include:

- notable concepts
- recurring themes
- questions requiring further investigation
- potential extraction candidates
- relationships to existing framework components

Observation capture is exploratory rather than authoritative.

---

12. Session Summary

Each Research Session should conclude with an editorial summary.

The summary should record:

- principal insights
- significant themes
- areas requiring additional research
- anticipated extraction priorities
- overall editorial assessment

The summary provides continuity between research and extraction.

---

13. Extraction Planning

Following completion of the session, the editor determines whether extraction should proceed.

Possible outcomes include:

- no extraction required
- targeted extraction
- comprehensive extraction
- deferred investigation
- framework transliteration
- additional research required

Extraction is a separate editorial activity governed by RCG-002.

---

14. Session Completion

A Research Session is considered complete when:

- the source has been sufficiently understood
- session objectives have been addressed
- observations have been recorded
- an editorial summary has been prepared
- extraction requirements have been identified

Completion does not imply that all knowledge has been extracted.

It signifies readiness for the next stage of the editorial process.

---

15. Relationship to the Editorial Pipeline

Research Sessions occupy the first operational stage following source registration.

Source Record
        ↓
Research Session
        ↓
Extraction
        ↓
Knowledge Article
        ↓
Framework Candidate
        ↓
Editorial Review

The quality of subsequent editorial work depends upon the quality of the Research Session.

---

Operational Statement

«A Research Session is not conducted to produce immediate framework. It is conducted to develop informed editorial understanding. Only after understanding has been established should reusable knowledge be extracted, synthesized, and considered for incorporation into the Unified Composition Guide.»
