# RCG-005 — Canonical Publication & Version Governance Standard

**Unified Composition Guide (UCG) Research Catalogue**

**Title:** Canonical Publication & Version Governance Standard  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Canonical Foundational Governance  
**Authority Level:** Core Operational Standard

---

## 1. Purpose

This standard establishes the governance principles by which research assets become canonical components of the Unified Composition Guide Research Catalogue.

It defines publication as an editorial act rather than a repository operation.

Its purpose is to ensure that institutional knowledge enters the canonical corpus through a consistent, traceable, and governed process.

---

## 2. Governing Principle

Publication is the formal recognition that an editorial asset has achieved canonical status.

Repository storage alone does not constitute publication.

Canonical publication requires governance approval, editorial readiness, and architectural integration.

---

## 3. Scope

This standard applies to all canonical research assets, including:

- Governance Documents
- Knowledge Articles
- Framework Candidates
- Canonical Framework Modules
- Subject Library entries
- Operational standards
- Supporting metadata

Operational procedures for publication are governed separately by the RIP series.

---

## 4. Canonical Status

Research assets progress through identifiable publication states.

Typical states include:

- Working Draft
- Editorial Review
- Approved
- Canonical
- Superseded
- Archived

Only Canonical assets represent the authoritative institutional position.

---

## 5. Publication Criteria

Before publication, an asset should satisfy the following criteria:

- editorially complete
- evidence-supported
- architecturally consistent
- appropriately classified
- correctly identified
- metadata complete
- relationships documented
- governance compliant

Publication signifies that the asset is suitable for institutional reliance.

---

## 6. Version Governance

Every canonical asset shall maintain an explicit version history.

Version identifiers communicate the degree of editorial change.

General guidance:

- Major versions represent substantive architectural or conceptual revision.
- Minor versions represent meaningful editorial enhancement without altering primary intent.
- Patch versions represent corrections, clarifications, or maintenance updates.

Version history contributes to institutional traceability.

---

## 7. Canonical Authority

Only the current canonical version of an asset represents authoritative guidance.

Earlier versions remain valuable for historical reference and editorial traceability but shall not supersede the active canonical edition.

The Research Catalogue preserves historical continuity while maintaining a single authoritative version of each asset.

---

## 8. Supersession

Knowledge evolves through editorial refinement.

When a canonical asset is replaced:

- the previous version remains archived
- supersession is documented
- relationships are updated
- historical traceability is preserved

Supersession strengthens institutional continuity rather than replacing history.

---

## 9. Publication Integrity

Publication should preserve:

- editorial integrity
- architectural consistency
- relationship continuity
- metadata completeness
- version history

Canonical publication represents a governance commitment to long-term maintainability.

---

## 10. Repository Independence

Canonical status is independent of storage technology.

Whether published through GitHub, Google Drive, local repositories, or future systems, the governance requirements established by this standard remain unchanged.

Repository implementation shall support governance rather than define it.

---

## Foundational Statement

> Publication is not the act of storing information. Publication is the formal recognition that knowledge has satisfied the editorial, architectural, and governance standards necessary to become part of the enduring institutional record. Canonical publication preserves both the authority of the present and the traceability of the past.
