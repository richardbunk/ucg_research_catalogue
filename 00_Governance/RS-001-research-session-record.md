1. Session Scope

Describe the intended scope of this research session.

Questions to consider:

- What portion of the source will be examined?
- What questions are being investigated?
- Is this exploratory research or targeted investigation?
- Is this session part of framework transliteration?

---

2. Initial Orientation

Summarize first impressions of the source.

Consider:

- overall purpose
- intended audience
- organizational structure
- writing style
- apparent strengths
- apparent limitations

This section captures orientation rather than analysis.

---

3. Context

Describe the broader context of the source.

Possible considerations:

- historical setting
- technical discipline
- relationship to existing UCG knowledge
- related research
- assumptions made by the author

Context assists later interpretation.

---

4. Significant Themes

Identify the major themes encountered during the session.

Possible examples:

- recurring concepts
- methodologies
- editorial philosophies
- systems
- terminology
- patterns

At this stage themes remain descriptive rather than codified.

---

5. Editorial Observations

Record noteworthy editorial observations.

Examples may include:

- recurring ideas
- contradictions
- strengths
- weaknesses
- surprising insights
- potential relationships to existing framework

Observations are working notes.

They need not yet become extraction records.

8. Research Questions

Record questions that remain unresolved.

These may include:

- verification requirements
- conflicting evidence
- terminology requiring clarification
- additional sources to consult

Research often generates better questions than immediate answers.

---

9. Session Summary

Provide a concise editorial summary.

Suggested topics:

- principal insights
- significance of the source
- anticipated value
- recommended next steps

The summary should capture understanding rather than exhaustive detail.
