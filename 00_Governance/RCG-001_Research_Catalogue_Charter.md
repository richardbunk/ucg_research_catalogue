# RCG-001 — Research Catalogue Charter
## Unified Composition Guide (UCG) Research Catalogue

**Document ID:** RCG-001  
**Title:** Research Catalogue Charter  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Canonical (Foundational Governance)  
**Authority Level:** Core Governance Document

---

# 1. Purpose

The UCG Research Catalogue exists to transform research from a temporary activity into a governed institutional knowledge asset.

Rather than treating research as disposable reference material used only during a single writing project, the Research Catalogue establishes a permanent, curated library of synthesized knowledge that can be reused, expanded, verified, and cited across future work.

The Catalogue serves as the authoritative bridge between raw information and compositional knowledge.

---

# 2. Mission

The mission of the Research Catalogue is to:

- preserve valuable research
- eliminate repeated investigation of established topics
- improve consistency across projects
- provide authoritative reference material for UCG composition
- enable cumulative institutional knowledge
- support long-form continuity across years of work

Research becomes an enduring organizational asset rather than an isolated project artifact.

---

# 3. Guiding Principles

The Research Catalogue operates according to several governing principles.

## 3.1 Knowledge Over Collection

The objective is not to collect information.

The objective is to synthesize information into usable knowledge.

Raw source material is temporary.

Curated understanding is permanent.

---

## 3.2 Research Must Be Reusable

A research entry should be written so that it remains useful outside the original project that created it.

Articles should answer enduring questions rather than temporary ones whenever practical.

---

## 3.3 Authority Through Evidence

Research conclusions should be supported by identifiable evidence.

Assertions should distinguish between:

- established fact
- interpretation
- inference
- speculation
- opinion

The reader should always understand the confidence level of a conclusion.

---

## 3.4 Neutrality

Research entries are descriptive rather than persuasive.

The Catalogue documents reality as accurately as possible.

It does not advocate unless advocacy is explicitly part of the source material being documented.

---

## 3.5 Evolution Rather Than Replacement

Knowledge evolves.

Research entries should be revised through controlled versioning rather than rewritten without history.

Earlier interpretations remain historically valuable.

---

# 4. Scope

The Research Catalogue may include any subject that supports present or future work.

Examples include:

- historical studies
- technical documentation
- artistic research
- linguistic references
- cultural studies
- business intelligence
- engineering
- writing theory
- scientific concepts
- software documentation
- historical recipes
- construction practices
- visual reference systems

The Catalogue is intentionally discipline-independent.

---

# 5. Research Hierarchy

Research progresses through increasing levels of refinement.

## Level 1 — Sources

Original material.

Examples:

- books
- papers
- interviews
- manuals
- websites
- archival documents

---

## Level 2 — Notes

Observations extracted from individual sources.

These remain tied to specific references.

---

## Level 3 — Synthesis

Information from multiple sources merged into coherent understanding.

Contradictions are identified and discussed.

---

## Level 4 — Research Article

A formal knowledge article intended for long-term reuse.

This becomes part of the Catalogue.

---

## Level 5 — Canon

Research that has undergone editorial review and is accepted as authoritative within the UCG ecosystem.

---

# 6. Research Article Requirements

A Catalogue article should generally include:

- title
- research objective
- scope
- historical or technical background
- synthesized findings
- unresolved questions
- confidence assessment
- primary references
- related Catalogue entries
- revision history

Not every article requires every section, but these represent the preferred structure.

---

# 7. Evidence Classification

Evidence should be categorized whenever practical.

Typical classifications include:

**Primary**

Original documents, firsthand records, direct observation.

**Secondary**

Scholarly interpretation or analysis.

**Tertiary**

Compilations, encyclopedias, summaries.

**Operational**

Practical experience or tested procedure.

**Experimental**

Unverified or developing information requiring further validation.

---

# 8. Confidence Levels

Research findings should indicate confidence.

Suggested levels:

- Confirmed
- Strong Evidence
- Probable
- Plausible
- Speculative
- Unknown

Confidence communicates certainty rather than importance.

---

# 9. Relationship to UCG

The Research Catalogue is not itself a writing framework.

Instead, it supplies verified knowledge to multiple UCG systems.

It informs:

- Author Personas
- Context Matrices
- Worldbuilding
- Product Registries
- Historical settings
- Technical writing
- Educational content
- Brand knowledge
- Visual authority systems
- Editorial planning

The Catalogue is therefore an upstream authority for composition.

---

# 10. Relationship to Source Material

Source material remains separate from Catalogue entries.

The Catalogue should not become an archive of copied documents.

Instead, it records:

- synthesized understanding
- important quotations where appropriate
- citations
- relationships between ideas
- editorial observations

The goal is knowledge, not duplication.

---

# 11. Editorial Responsibilities

Editors are responsible for:

- maintaining factual integrity
- identifying conflicting evidence
- preventing unsupported conclusions
- preserving revision history
- ensuring clarity
- improving discoverability

Editors do not alter conclusions without supporting evidence.

---

# 12. Versioning

Research evolves through explicit versions.

Minor revisions:

- formatting
- clarification
- additional references

Major revisions:

- new interpretations
- substantial evidence
- structural reorganization

Historical versions should remain recoverable.

---

# 13. Canonical Status

A Catalogue entry becomes canonical only after editorial review.

Canonical status indicates:

- internally reviewed
- sufficiently supported
- appropriate for future reuse

Canonical does not imply absolute truth.

It indicates accepted authority within the UCG knowledge ecosystem pending future evidence.

---

# 14. Long-Term Vision

The Research Catalogue is intended to become an expanding institutional library whose value increases over time.

Each completed article should reduce future research effort while improving consistency, depth, and authority across all UCG-assisted work.

Its purpose is not merely to answer today's question, but to preserve today's understanding for tomorrow's projects.

---

# Foundational Statement

> The UCG Research Catalogue exists to preserve knowledge as a durable institutional asset. Through disciplined synthesis, evidence-based authorship, governed revision, and cumulative organization, it transforms isolated research into a continuously expanding body of reusable understanding that strengthens every future composition.
